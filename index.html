About this work

Eugène Boudin (1824–1898) is widely regarded as a direct precursor to Impressionism, celebrated for his luminous coastal scenes and confident plein-air technique. This work, from his mature period, exemplifies the sky drama and tidal atmosphere that drive collector demand. Specialist French houses consistently achieve a 20–30% premium over international majors for Boudin coastal works.
